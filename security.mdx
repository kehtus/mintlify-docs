**Audit report:** [View the Beosin audit report](https://beosin.com/audits/Proportion_202603161420.pdf)

## Continuous Review

The codebase is regularly reviewed manually and re-evaluated using modern AI-powered security analysis tools. AI serves as an additional layer of continuous review, not as a replacement for independent audits or expert analysis.

## Administrative Security

Critical administrative actions are protected by a 3-of-5 multisig and timelocks. Administrative authority is restricted, and protocol parameters can only be changed within predefined safe bounds. A single compromised key cannot execute privileged actions.

### Production Timelock Schedule

| Minimum delay | Changes covered |
| --- | --- |
| **72 hours** | `MainVault` and `ChallengeVault` implementation upgrades; `transferOwnership` and other governance-control changes; migrations that replace a core Vault implementation. |
| **48 hours** | `queueFactoryChange` / `executeFactoryChange` for funded accounts; `proposeFactory` / `setFactory` for Challenge checkout; trusted integration changes such as `setRelayer`, `setForwarder`, `setMainVault`, and `setChallengeVault`; `setRiskConfig`, `setGlobalConfig`, market additions, leverage increases, and other changes that increase protocol risk. |

<Note>
  Defensive actions that reduce risk may be executed immediately. These include pausing new activations or purchases, canceling a queued change, disabling checkout, removing a market, and lowering leverage. Unpausing or increasing risk remains subject to the applicable timelock.
</Note>

## Key Management

Particular attention is given to key management, signer security, protection against social engineering, and reducing the risk of human error.
