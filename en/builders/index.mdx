> The builder owns the product. Proportion provides the funded layer.

For a builder, the funded account is the most capital-intensive and infrastructure-heavy part of a prop firm. Proportion provides this layer as infrastructure: capital, on-chain risk enforcement, and settlement. Challenges, evaluation, brand, marketing, and trader selection remain the builder's product.

Proportion is API-compatible with Hyperliquid Builder Codes, so builders can integrate it into an existing trading frontend with minimal client-side changes. The builder buys access to protocol capital and pays for it from challenge revenue or other selection products.

To become a builder, they deposit into the LP vault. The deposit works as LP capital and earns yield, so the effective cost of activating an FA is lower than the nominal amount. The same deposit gives the builder skin in the game: activation fees are designed to compensate expected drawdown risk, but realized losses can still reduce pool yield and `sharePrice`.

## What Proportion Gives Builders

A funded account is the base primitive Proportion provides: an SDK call creates an isolated on-chain account with capital, drawdown limits, and automatic risk enforcement. The FA smart contract belongs to the user within protocol rules.

| Builder layer — product | Protocol layer |
|-------------------------|----------------|
| Challenge and evaluation | Capital from the LP vault |
| Trader selection and onboarding | On-chain risk engine (drawdown, leverage, allowed perps) |
| Brand, marketing, pricing | Settlement: fees, subscriptions, profit split 80/13/7 |

Boundary: everything before FA activation is handled by the builder; everything after activation is enforced by the protocol.

### Builder = LP Too

To become a builder, you need to deposit at least $10,000 USDC into the LP vault. This deposit:

- Gives a funding quota: 10x leverage ($10k → up to $100k of active trader capital)
- Makes the builder part of the LP pool — shares participate in gains and losses across all FAs in the protocol, not only their own

The LP pool earns from activation fees and trader profits. Drawdown from traders who hit their limits reduces pool yield. The builder has skin in the game twice: in their own product and in the health of the whole ecosystem.
