## Problems Proportion Solves

- **Opaque rules and blocked payouts** in classic prop — rules and splits are encoded in the protocol, with payouts handled through the on-chain flow
- **B-book conflict of interest** — builders and the protocol are aligned with trader profit, not trader losses
- **Counterparty risk** — funded accounts are non-custodial, and protocol rules prevent arbitrary payout denial by the prop firm
- **High barrier to launching a prop firm** — Proportion provides capital, risk enforcement, and settlement without requiring builders to run their own contract stack
- **Fragmented onchain capital** — one Vault coordinates liquidity instead of separate pools for every firm
- **Lack of DeFi access to prop-industry economics** — Vault shares create a composable primitive for LPs

## What Each Side Gets

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Trader">
    - Capital access without KYC or artificial rules
    - A-book execution on real Hyperliquid liquidity — real trades with real capital
    - 80% PnL hardcoded by the protocol
    - The FA belongs to the trader within protocol rules; the prop firm cannot arbitrarily block payouts
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Builder">
    - Infrastructure for funded accounts: capital from the Vault, on-chain risk enforcement, and settlement
    - Builder leverage 10x: a $10k deposit can fund up to $100k of trader capital
    - Revenue on three levels: challenge margin, 85% of subscription fees, LP yield from the pool
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="LP">
    - Yield from the ecosystem: 90% of activation fees and 13% of trader profits
    - No need to pick builders or traders — sharePrice reflects Vault P&L and can increase or decrease
    - Composable shares (potentially for DeFi integrations)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
